# Data Champions Live — Fireside Chat with Lina Haddad

In this 35-minute conversation, Lina Haddad walks through the operating model her civic tech team created to hard-wire accountability into a national open data platform. She breaks down the core tenets behind their stewardship playbook and the practical rituals that keep community stakeholders engaged.

## Highlights from the conversation

- **Purpose-built stewardship metrics** — Lina details how the team aligned their product roadmap around clearly articulated public interest outcomes.
- **Community review cadences** — Quarterly trust labs bring together ministries, civil society, and affected communities to evaluate how datasets are curated and governed.
- **Iterating on safe access** — The team uses tiered release schedules, dynamic licensing, and rapid-response review boards to balance innovation with privacy.

## Why it matters

For governments and NGOs scaling digital infrastructure, Lina’s approach showcases how design-driven rituals can sustain legitimacy. The interview also surfaces low-lift templates you can adapt for your own stewardship councils, consent journeys, and data sharing agreements.

> _"Accountability cannot just be a PDF. We rewired our sprints so that every dataset is launched with a stewardship storyboard, testing plan, and community sign-off."_
